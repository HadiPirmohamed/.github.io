Residential Loft Conversion

Freelance Work



An interior renovation and loft conversion for a new-build detached house in Watford. The project consisted of designing a new study, an extended living/dining room, an additional bedroom, en-suite and extra storage space. This was achieved through creating an open plan layout on the ground floor to create an airy atmosphere whilst also allowing space for a 2 person study. Bedroom 3 was relocated to the loft to create space for a walk-in closet in the master bedroom and a staircase to access the loft. Finally, the loft consisted of bedroom 3 and another master bedroom with a walk-in closet and en-suite. All design considerations where made to ensure the project was affordable and within the clients budget.

Jewellery Quarter Design Project: Connect

BSc Architecture (RIBA Part I), Year 3



A community centre located at the edge of Jewellery Quarter. Connect is a space where the public can learn about the history of the area and learn a new skill (making jewellery/small metal objects). The project was inspired by the area, hence, having the proposal connect with its urban context, exploring connections within the facade materials and most importantly, promoting connection and collaboration of the building users.

Coventry Charterhouse

BSc Architecture (RIBA Part I), Year 1



An extension to the historic Coventry Charterhouse, where individuals can socialise in the educational space and cafe whilst learning about the history of Coventry. Design features such as the location, building size and design were reflected on during the conceptual stages to ensure the importance of the Charterhouse was kept, whilst also enticing individuals to use the space.
